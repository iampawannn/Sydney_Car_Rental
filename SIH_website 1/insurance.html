Sydney Car Rental Insurance Policy



Effective Date: January 1, 2024

Policy Number: SCR123456789

1. Introduction
This insurance policy ("Policy") is provided by Sydney Car Rental ("Company") to customers ("Insured") who rent vehicles from the Company. The Policy outlines the terms and conditions under which coverage is provided during the rental period.
2. Definitions
Vehicle: Any car, SUV, van, or other motor vehicle rented from the Company.
Rental Agreement: The contract signed by the Insured and the Company outlining the terms of the vehicle rental.
Loss: Any physical damage to or theft of the Vehicle.
Accident: An unexpected and unintentional event resulting in damage to the Vehicle.
Deductible: The amount the Insured must pay out-of-pocket before insurance coverage applies.
3. Coverage
3.1 Collision Damage Waiver (CDW)
The CDW covers the cost of repairing or replacing the Vehicle in case of an accident, collision, or other incidents causing physical damage to the Vehicle, subject to the terms and conditions of this Policy.

Coverage Limit: Up to the actual cash value of the Vehicle.
Deductible: AUD 500 per incident.
3.2 Theft Protection (TP)
TP covers the cost of replacing the Vehicle in case of theft or attempted theft.

Coverage Limit: Up to the actual cash value of the Vehicle.
Deductible: AUD 750 per incident.
3.3 Third-Party Liability (TPL)
TPL provides coverage for legal liabilities arising from bodily injury or property damage caused to third parties while operating the Vehicle.

Coverage Limit: AUD 5,000,000 per incident.
Deductible: None.
3.4 Personal Accident Insurance (PAI)
PAI covers medical expenses for the Insured and passengers in the Vehicle in the event of an accident.

Coverage Limit: AUD 10,000 per person.
Deductible: None.
4. Exclusions
This Policy does not cover:

Any loss or damage occurring while the Vehicle is driven by someone not authorized under the Rental Agreement.
Damage caused by driving under the influence of alcohol, drugs, or other illegal substances.
Intentional damage or gross negligence by the Insured.
Loss or damage to personal belongings inside the Vehicle.
Use of the Vehicle for illegal purposes or activities.
Any pre-existing damage to the Vehicle noted at the time of rental.
5. Claims Procedure
In the event of a loss, accident, or theft, the Insured must:

Notify the Company immediately and provide a detailed report of the incident.
File a police report within 24 hours if the Vehicle is stolen or if required by law.
Cooperate with the Company and insurance adjusters during the investigation and claims process.
Submit any required documents, including a completed claim form, police report, and any other relevant information.
6. General Provisions
6.1 Termination of Coverage
Coverage under this Policy will terminate:

At the end of the rental period as specified in the Rental Agreement.
If the Rental Agreement is terminated by either party.
If the Insured fails to comply with the terms and conditions of this Policy or the Rental Agreement.
6.2 Subrogation
In the event of any payment under this Policy, the Company is subrogated to all the rights of the Insured against any third party liable for the loss.

6.3 Governing Law
This Policy shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of New South Wales, Australia.

6.4 Amendments
The Company reserves the right to amend or update this Policy at any time. Any changes will be effective upon posting the updated Policy on the Company’s website.
